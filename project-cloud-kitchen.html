360° Cloud Kitchen

Revolutionizing the Culinary Landscape

Description

Cloud kitchens are delivery-only restaurant with no physical dine-in space. The 360° Cloud Kitchen Solution ensures seamless delivery is prioritized, and profits are maximized. Restauranters can manage their entire business by controlling orders, inventory, menu, kitchen operations and more.

Problem Statement

  • Manual order management, outdated POS systems, and paper-based processes lead to inefficiencies in kitchen & restaurant operations.
  • Customers are increasingly seeking convenience, variety, and personalized experiences.

What competitors do

The successful practices of competitors: Square, Toast and Loyverse were analyzed to get an understanding of base functionalities and the industry standard.

Solution Deliverable

The solution has two seperate but inter-connected systems: the Kitchen Display System(KDS) and the Restaurant Management System which includes the Point of Sales(POS). Both are device agnostic web-apps.

This case study will be presented as a short story,
hope you enjoy the ride.

The Setting

Lily's Meridian is a cloud kitchen success story based in Dubai, they've been consistently serving delicious meals since 2020 and only plans on growing in the future.

The Characters

Luca - Manager, wearer of many hats. Handles orders and basically everything else.
Sara - Devout customer. Busy product designer who regularly relies on Lily's Meridian for lunch.
Jacob - Chef since 2016. Cooking wizard on a journey to perfect his art.

It's 10:00 am, Luca just logged into the new app
his boss installed to help everyone at work.

"Ring, Ring!", Luca picks up the call, it's Sara their regular customer, she prefers placing orders via phone.
Luca pulls up the checkout screen & easily adds items(including modifiers!) from different menus and categories, to place her order. He really values the screen layout customization feature.

The order just popped up on the Kitchen Display System(KDS) as a new ticket. Jacob got to it first, he accepted it & started prepping.
His kitchen mates love the new KDS, they can hold tickets and recall finished ones, he personally finds the game like visuals really cool.

After the first week, Jacob got a hang of the ticket structure.

By 11:00 am, there's already a bunch of orders lined up from various delivery platforms. From preparation to delivery, Luca tracks the orders to maintain quality.
He conveniently updates Sara's order status, as it just got delivered.

Sara's lunch got delivered! She's really satisfied with the prompt service and impeccable food.

Luca just noticed a refund request for order sale number 'CK93', he navigates to transactions, where order sales, refunds, and payments can be viewed. He opens 'CK93' & issues refund.

It's 2:00pm, peak rush hour, Jacob opens the 'All day view' to cook similar items in bulk.

From the kitchen, Jacob tells Luca that 'Penne Pasta with Red Sauce' from the Lunch menu is unavailable. Luca pops the menu open, and updates the item status. He takes a moment to appreciate how fluid customization, updates, and presentation of menus items are.

It's now 8:00pm, Lily's Meridian is closed for the day. Luca can finally track the inventory & stock-take(record wastage & surplus) to ensure timely restocking.

Style Guide

The interface was designed for touch screens because KDS and POS UIs are usually touch based. Style inspiration was taken from games like Elden Ring and Far Cry.

The search for perfection

Designing an experience is rarely a linear task, the result you witnessed above were attained after numerous iterations starting from storyboard to wireframes to rejected prototypes.
